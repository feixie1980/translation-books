Chapter 29: The Attack Pattern of Sydney Gyn's Resident

The very first words Sydney Gyn heard from behind her, in the midst of her terror, stirred something unexpected — a faint, fleeting sense of relief.

"I knew you'd come back to life long ago,"

Her own voice drifted up from somewhere behind and above her head, thick as a cloud of miasma, low and unhurried. "You're so easy to fool — nothing like me at all."

Good, Sydney Gyn thought, spinning on one knee and twisting her body around in the same motion. Thank god its first words were a waste of breath.

What did it matter that it had noticed early? One way or another, the two of them were always going to end up here, fighting for their lives.

She had already steeled herself for this — and yet the moment "Sydney Gyn" flashed into her field of vision, she couldn't help it: her breath caught, as if someone had shoved her hard in the chest.

Compared to the "Sydney Gyn" she remembered, the Resident looked somehow more vivid, more real: a forehead glinting with tiny beads of sweat in the firelight, a strand of golden-brown hair stuck to her temple; the fine down on her cheeks, the warm breath spilling from between her lips…

Those eyes, burning so bright it looked like nothing else was left inside them.

This was Sydney Gyn. This was herself — and yet she herself was standing outside that self, arm raised high, bringing a dark shape crashing down toward her face.

Sydney Gyn threw herself sideways at the last possible moment. A thunderous impact exploded beside her ear as the exhibit was bitten deep, gouged into a massive crater; amid the shower of splinters she squeezed her eyes shut and rolled frantically across the floor, already unable to tell how many wooden shards had stabbed into her face and the palms of her hands.

"Just die already — there can only be one Sydney Gyn in this world!"

The voice — strange, and yet entirely her own — rang out in wave after wave through the exhibition hall; with a grunt of effort, "Sydney Gyn" wrenched a fire axe free from deep within the sculpture of wooden slats.

When had it...?

She didn't even finish the thought. Sydney Gyn sprang to her feet and bolted — only to remember, too late, that she had taken her shoes off earlier. Her feet were bare except for socks, and on the slick exhibition hall floor she had almost no traction at all.

In the instant before she went sprawling, she committed: arms forward, body flat, she launched herself into a racing dive — since she'd opened her eyes again, she seemed to have spent more time face-down on the floor than standing upright.

The fire axe hit the floor tiles with a muffled thud, burying itself deep — barely one step behind her.

Sydney Gyn had never in her life peeled off a sock so fast.

While the axe was still stuck in the floor, she ripped the sock off and flung it straight at her other self's face, aimed right at her eyes. Like any normal person, the Resident had no desire to take a sock to the eyeballs, and jerked its head aside.

Sydney Gyn would have loved to keep the momentum going, but there was no time to pull off the other sock — the Resident had already closed the distance in one stride and was hauling the axe up high above her.

Almost on instinct, she drove her foot hard into the Resident's ankle.

Once a Resident had replicated a human form, it was nothing more than an ordinary human body; when one person kicked another's shinbone with every ounce of strength they had, the pain was real, and the Resident stumbled back two lurching steps. Sydney Gyn seized the opening, scrambled upright, and ran — one foot gripping, one foot sliding.

But even as she ran, her mind kept drifting back to that ankle she'd just kicked.

It was something she had never once thought about before: what shoes was the Resident wearing?

She already knew that when a Resident replicated someone, it couldn't reproduce things like backpacks or phones. But what about clothes? What about shoes?

In that split-second glimpse during the life-or-death scramble just now…

"Wait!"

Sydney Gyn knew perfectly well that with one bare foot she had no chance of outrunning the Resident — and the more chances she gave it to talk, the more danger she was in. She spun around and called out: "What are you wearing on your feet?"

The other "Sydney Gyn," teeth gritted, bearing down hard behind her, faltered for just a moment at the question — but it wasn't distracted. It brought the axe down first; only after Sydney Gyn barely dodged did it laugh between ragged breaths: "Trying to buy yourself time with small talk, are you?"

"Residents can't replicate backpacks — and they can't replicate clothing either!"

Terrified it would catch up, Sydney Gyn rushed on: "You copied my appearance, but the shoes you're wearing are still your own. You must have my memories of what I was wearing when I came to the Nest today — they're in your head, aren't they? Look down. Think about it. Do the clothes in your memory match what's actually on your body?"

"Sydney Gyn" blinked, and — incredibly — actually looked down at her clothes.

Even a Resident of the Nest couldn't go around naked; they always had something on.

This one, perhaps because it was a Vulture — always ready to take someone else's shape at a moment's notice — wore plainly practical, versatile black trousers and black shoes. You couldn't really blame Sydney Gyn for never having noticed; when a face identical to your own surfaces out of the darkness, you don't tend to pay much attention to what shoes it has on.

"You're right, actually," it said when it raised its head again, and its voice was clear and composed: "When I enter the Nest at night, I always wear all black so I won't draw attention. If you thought you could trick me with that, it's almost funny."

It swung the fire axe loosely at its side and gave a small, light kick with the toe of one shoe.

"I can even tell you that I bought these shoes on sale at Bloomingdale's — because I thought, anything I wear into the Nest just needs to hold up, there's no point paying full price for something that looks good."

Now it was Sydney Gyn's turn to freeze. That logic was unmistakably, purely her own. She could even recall several pieces of clothing she had bought for exactly the same reason.

"And you?" The Resident smiled at her. A strand of hair caught on its lips, framing its teeth. "If you really have my memories, you'll find that it's your clothes that don't add up."

Sydney Gyn looked down involuntarily, her gaze dropping to the khaki joggers she was wearing.

She remembered pulling on these very trousers before she climbed out the window… but where had they come from?

Most people could more or less remember where they'd bought their own clothes — but these joggers felt as though they had been pulled onto her by someone else just a few seconds ago, so unfamiliar they left her mind blank.

Why was there no memory of ever buying them?

"It seems that after a Resident replicates its original, it smooths and polishes the memories — touches up any details that don't match reality, papers over the cracks… That's why you're so utterly convinced that you're the real me."

While she stood there with her head bowed, staring blankly at the trousers, the other "Sydney Gyn" had been slowly closing in; its voice, its warmth, its smell, and those feet in their black shoes were steadily encroaching on her existence.

"Residents can't be killed — I've heard that."

It said quietly, "But I'll feel better once I've taken away your ability to move first… I'd imagine that copying a human form has a time limit for you, doesn't it? When the time runs out and you still haven't gone to Blackmoor City to replace me, you'll revert back to your Resident form, won't you?"

Wait — what was it saying?

Something stirred deep in her mind, like a quiet needle-prick, and a grain-of-rice speck of light broke through — but the light only flickered for an instant before heavier, more urgent shadows closed over it again.

She neither heard nor saw it coming — it was something beyond the five senses, some instinct entirely her own, that made Sydney Gyn suddenly duck low and lunge forward in the same heartbeat the fire axe swung down toward her skull, wrapping her arms around the Resident and driving it clean off its feet.

The Resident's arm, coiled with the full force of its swing, came down hard across her ear and the side of her head; but the axe itself — the part that would have killed her — scraped past her shoulder at last and spun away, clattering onto the floor tiles a short distance off.

The two of them crashed down together, and the back of the Resident's head struck the floor with a sound that clenched every organ in Sydney Gyn's body.

"You copied my form," it still managed to force out, struggling, "so you too —"

Before it could finish the sentence, Sydney Gyn had already yanked the towel she'd tucked into the back waistband of her trousers and crammed it at its mouth.

The Resident twisted its head left and right to avoid it; the towel didn't make it in cleanly, but it did stop the sentence from being completed.

Sydney Gyn didn't dare let go. Gritting her teeth, she absorbed punch after punch landing on her body, both hands holding the long towel taut — and when the Resident's lips parted for just an instant, she shoved it deep into its mouth. The towel stretched like a rope across her other self's cheek, forcing its tongue back into the depths of its throat, leaving it capable of nothing but muffled, wordless moaning.

"I think… I think I understand now…" She stared at the face distorted beneath the towel and said quietly: "I think I've figured out your attack pattern. You really are the Resident."

Yesterday I got to the hotel, finished dinner, typed for a while longer, and then simply couldn't hold on any longer and fell asleep… Got up early this morning to keep going, otherwise I'd feel guilty all day.

This hotel stay was basically mooching off Qidian — you know how they had that promotional event for the Joy of Life drama production? Nothing to do with me, but I sort of snuck along for the ride, kind of like a stowaway situation… I keep getting this vague, nervous feeling that someone's going to audit the books afterward and make me pay for my room. Someone from Qidian told me Maoni was staying there too, and — rounding up in the boldest possible way — that puts you and Maoni in the same league.

Rounding up with strides that wide, you could step from the Huangpu River all the way to Tokyo in one go…
