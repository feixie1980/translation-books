Chapter 3: Even If You Fall Into a Ghost Story, You Still Have to Go to Work
Amidst the living hell of a ghost story where people were dropping dead, a single survival ticket had suddenly appeared.
Just like in the movies.
And it wasn't an illusion either.
'If you get off with the broadcasted lost item, you will be guided out of the station the moment you meet a staff member...'
It was an undeniable ray of hope.
The only problem was that the lost item mentioned in the announcement was absurdly grotesque.
"What did it just say? A male in his twenties...?"
"The left eyeball of a Type-A adult male."
Go Yeongeun hung her head with a hollow expression.
"Type A probably refers to... a blood type, right?"
"Yes..."
A suffocating silence fell over the carriage. Everyone already seemed paralyzed with fear.
'...Should I just let this pass?'
Everyone could survive anyway if we simply disembarked at the correct station. If someone managed to escape early for no reason, it might ruin the collective mood and cause unnecessary chaos.
But things wouldn't go that smoothly.
"S-Still, wouldn't it be better to look for it?"
"Right. We have no idea what might happen next..."
The new recruits had become so hyper-aware of the announcements that they simply couldn't ignore them.
'...Let's just see how this plays out for now.'
"How old are you, by any chance...?"
"Ah, I'm in my thirties."
"You look young for your age. Ah, I'm Type B, so I don't qualify."
"Me too..."
The recruits began checking with one another to see if anyone fit the criteria of a 'Type-A male in his twenties.'
But nobody perfectly matched the conditions, and soon enough, the question came around to me.
"What about you, Mr. Kim Soleum...?"
I replied simply, "I'm Type AB."
It was a total lie. I was actually Type A.
'But there's no reason for me to reveal that I fit the bill.'
If I stood to benefit or lose from this scenario, they might think my future advice was driven by self-interest, making them impossible to convince later when it truly mattered.
"I see. Then what about Mr. Baek Saheon...?"
"Hold on."
Suddenly, the man sitting across from me raised his hand with a solemn expression.
"Actually... I perfectly fit the criteria!"
"Ah!"
He spoke with an incredibly grave tone, likely because he feared he might get killed if he didn't secure his escape immediately.
'Though he really didn't need to worry about that.'
And the reason for his sudden confession became clear immediately.
[This stop is Wrath, Wrath Station.]
The intercom announced our arrival at a new station—the very place where a station worker would be waiting to collect the lost property.
Tension lined the face of the recruit who had just volunteered.
"Is there no one else?"
"Yes... it seems so."
"Oh dear," Baek Saheon sighed from his seat next to me after listening to the conversation, before turning to the volunteer.
[The doors are on your right...]
"You said you're Type A?"
"Yes, yes."
"A shame."
And then, a fist flew.
"…?!"
A fist slammed ruthlessly right into the left eye of the recruit who had just outed himself as a Type-A twenty-something, accompanied by a sickening pop.
Baek Saheon had struck the man with the corner of the smartphone gripped tightly in his hand. He had swung with enough force to deliberately rupture the eyeball.
"Gah—"
Before the man could even properly voice a scream, he collapsed. In that exact split second—
[The screen doors are opening.]
Baek Saheon lightly leaped over the fallen man and stepped out of the train.
"W-What just..."
"Ahhh!"
"What the hell is wrong with you?!"
The crowd finally broke out of their stupor and screamed in utter shock, a step too late.
Baek Saheon let out a snicker. "Idiots. If you can escape at the cost of a single eyeball, you'd better take the deal immediately!"
"…!"
"The train literally said we could disembark!"
As it turned out, Baek Saheon had accurately read between the lines of the intercom announcement.
-The passenger who retrieves the lost property must disembark at the next station and return the item to the station personnel.
Even with the hint to pay attention to the announcements, it was an incredibly audacious move.
"Then again... I guess I shouldn't expect that kind of resolve from people who rely on someone else to take responsibility for their lives."
"What?!"
Baek Saheon remained completely unbothered even while casually hurling insults. He seemed absolutely certain that nobody would dare follow him out.
And he was right. Everyone had already witnessed the horrific fate of those who disembarked at the wrong station.
"Th-That psycho..."
Go Yeongeun's face turned bright red with fury, but for me, a sudden wave of clarity washed over my mind as the pieces clicked together.
No wonder!
His alias was The Viper. The notorious exploits of Baek Saheon documented in the Records of Darkness Investigation wiki only made sense when attributed to a personality as cutthroat as this.
I had initially thought he was just a decent, green rookie who hadn't been corrupted by the corporate world yet, but it turned out he was just inherently a monster.
[The doors are closing.]
Through the closing glass, Baek Saheon waved at the interior of the car. He looked utterly delighted with himself for successfully securing an escape—even if it meant sacrificing his own left eye later.
'Wow...'
But here's the thing.
'He really didn't need to do all that.'
Taking advantage of the moment everyone's attention was locked onto Baek Saheon, I stealthily reached up toward the overhead luggage rack.
'I definitely caught a glimpse of it earlier.'

[Type A / Female / 27 / R]
This object right here was one of the lost property candidates.

Though, to be fair, there had been rare anomalies where a victim escaped by coincidentally sacrificing their own matching body part.
'And Baek Saheon was about to become one of those cases.'
But I wondered... how would he feel if he realized he never actually had to "surrender" his own left eye?
The platform screen doors were already sliding shut. Right before the train pulled away, I caught Baek Saheon's gaze for a split second as he turned around on the platform.
I deliberately held the lens case up against the glass door, turning it so the label was obscured. From his angle, only one thing would be vividly visible.
The floating eyeball.

But his realization came a moment too late.
[The train is now departing from Wrath Station.]
The subway chugged away.

'He probably thought the system would only accept a single lost item for the entire station.'

I turned back to the window.
Behind us, I heard a series of violent, frantic thuds rattling against the platform screen doors. And from the fading platform, the faint, distorted shriek of someone enduring unimaginable agony echoed through the tunnel.
[The lost property has been successfully turned over to the station personnel.]
